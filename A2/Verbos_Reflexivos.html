¿Qué es un Verbo Reflexivo?

The "Mirror" Effect

Un verbo es reflexivo cuando el sujeto y el objeto son la misma persona.

In English: The action "reflects" back to the person doing it. It’s like looking in a mirror. The person doing the action also receives the action.

Normal Verb

Yo lavo el auto.

(I wash the car)

Reflexive Verb

Yo me lavo.

(I wash myself)

Los Pronombres Reflexivos

How do I know?

Look at the Infinitive form in the dictionary. If it ends in -se, it is reflexive.

Lavar Lavarse

Why are they mandatory?

They indicate the direction of the action. Without the pronoun, the verb becomes "transitive" (action goes outwards to someone else).

Me lavo (Action stays here)

Singular

Yo ME myself
Tú TE yourself
Él / Ella SE him/herself

Plural

Nosotros NOS ourselves
Vosotros OS yourselves
Ellos / Uds. SE themselves

Pronoun Placement

Rule #1

One Conjugated Verb

The pronoun MUST go BEFORE the conjugated verb. It never goes after.

SE
VERB

Ella se levanta.

"She gets up."

Rule #2

Infinitive / Gerund

When you have two verbs (Helper + Infinitive), you have TWO OPTIONS. Both are 100% correct!

Option A: Before

Me quiero bañar.

OR / O

Option B: Attached

Quiero bañarme.

Teacher's Tip

Don't stress! Choose the option that feels easiest for you. Most English speakers prefer Option B because the pronoun goes at the end, similar to English.

Reflexive vs. Normal Verbs

The difference is the OBJECT. Who receives the action?

Type A

Normal Verb

The action goes OUT to another person/object.

Ana despierta al niño.

Ana wakes the child up.

Yo lavo la ropa.

I wash the clothes.

Type B

Reflexive Verb

The action comes BACK to the subject.

Ana se despierta.

Ana wakes (herself) up.

Yo me lavo.

I wash (myself).

Important Rule: Body & Clothes

The "English" Trap

In English, you say "I wash MY face".
But in Spanish, using possessives (mi, tu, su) here is redundant.

Yo me lavo MI cara.

Incorrect

The Spanish Way

Since the reflexive pronoun (me) already shows it happens to YOU, we just point to the object using articles (el, la, los, las).

Yo me lavo LA cara.

Correct

THE FORMULA

Reflexive Verb EL / LA / LOS / LAS Body Part
Me cepillo los dientes.
Se pone la chaqueta.

Change of Meaning

Watch out! Adding -se doesn't always mean "to yourself". Sometimes, it creates a completely new verb with a different meaning.

IR

To go (destination)

IRSE

To leave / To go away

"¡Me voy!" (I'm leaving!)

DORMIR

To sleep (action)

DORMIRSE

To fall asleep

"Me duermo en clase" (I fall asleep)

PONER

To put / place

PONERSE

To put on (clothes)

"Me pongo la camisa"

PARECER

To seem

PARECERSE (a)

To look like someone

"Me parezco a mi padre"

Key Takeaways

  • Pronoun goes before conjugated verb (Me lavo).
  • Use Articles (el/la), not Possessives (mi/tu) for body parts.
  • Watch out for "Transformers" (Ir vs. Irse).
