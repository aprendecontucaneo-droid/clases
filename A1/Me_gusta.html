El Verbo Gustar

Advanced A1 Lab

Understanding "Likes" and "Dislikes" in Spanish.

Mastering the backwards logic of Spanish emotion verbs.

The "Backwards" Logic

In English, we say:
"I like the book."

In Spanish, we don't say "I like". We say something closer to:

"To me is pleasing the book."
Me gusta el libro.

Notice: "The book" is actually the subject of the sentence!

The Ingredients: Pronouns

We do not use Yo, Tú, Él. We use Indirect Object Pronouns.

A mí → Me (to me)

A ti → Te (to you)

A él/ella/Ud. → Le (to him/her/you)

A nosotros → Nos (to us)

A vosotros → Os (to you all)

A ellos/ellas/Uds. → Les (to them)

Rule #1: Singular Objects

If the thing you like is singular (one thing), use GUSTA.

Me gusta el café.
Te gusta la música.
Nos gusta la clase.

Practice: Singular

Fill in the blank with gusta.

1. Me el chocolate.
2. ¿Te la película?
3. Nos la idea.
4. Le el libro.

Rule #2: Plural Objects

If the thing you like is plural (more than one), use GUSTAN.

The 'n' is added because the subject is plural.

Me gustan los libros.
Te gustan las flores.

Practice: Plural

Fill in the blank with gustan.

1. Me los perros.
2. ¿Te tus clases?
3. Nos los tacos.
4. Les los deportes.

Quick Check: Singular vs Plural

Choose gusta or gustan based on the noun.

1. Me el hotel. (Singular)
2. Me los hoteles. (Plural)
3. Le las fiestas.
4. Le la fiesta.

Rule #3: Verbs (Actions)

When talking about activities (verbs), ALWAYS use the singular GUSTA.

Even if you list multiple verbs, keep it singular.

Me gusta viajar. (I like to travel)

Me gusta escribir y leer. (I like to write and read)

Practice: Activities

Complete the sentences with gusta (verbs) or gustan (plural nouns).

1. Nos bailar salsa.
2. Me los zapatos nuevos.
3. Te estudiar español.
4. Les correr por la mañana.

The Ambiguity of "Le"

The pronoun Le can mean: To him, To her, To you.

To avoid confusion, we add a "Clarifier" (Prepositional Phrase) at the start.

A él le gusta el vino.

A ella le gusta el vino.

A usted le gusta el vino.

A María le gusta el vino.

Emphasis for Me & Te

Even though "Me" and "Te" are clear, we can add phrases for emphasis.

A mí me gusta el café.

"As for me, I like coffee."

A ti te gustan los gatos.

"As for you, you like cats."

The Negative "No"

To say you don't like something, simply place NO before the pronoun.

No me gusta cocinar.

No te gustan las arañas.

A ella no le gusta el frío.

Verb: Encantar (To love)

Works exactly like Gustar but stronger.
"To be enchanting to..."

Me encanta viajar. (I love to travel)
Me encantan los viajes. (I love trips)

Note: Encantar is strong! Don't usually use it with "No".

Verb: Interesar (To interest)

Is it interesting to you?

Me interesa la historia. (History interests me)
Nos interesan los idiomas. (Languages interest us)
¿Te interesa aprender?

Verb: Doler (To hurt)

Used for body parts.
"To me hurts the head."

Important: Use "el/la/los/las" (articles), NOT "mi/tu" (possessives) for body parts.

Me duele la cabeza.

Me duelen los pies.

Verb: Molestar (To bother)

Does it annoy you?

Me molesta el ruido. (The noise bothers me)
¿Te molestan los mosquitos?
A él no le molesta nada.
