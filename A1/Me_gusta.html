El verbo gustar

Nivel A2 · Me gusta / Me gustan

Likes and dislikes in Spanish. Learn the "backwards" logic and practice with fun exercises.

The "backwards" logic

Introduction (English): In Spanish we don't say "I like the book". The thing you like is the subject of the sentence. We say something like "To me is pleasing the book."

English: "I like the book."

Spanish (literal): "To me is pleasing the book."

Me gusta el libro.

The book (el libro) is the subject. Me = to me (indirect object). Gusta agrees with the subject (singular).

Indirect object pronouns (IOP)

Instruction: With gustar we use these pronouns (to me, to you…), not Yo / Tú / Él. Learn this table.

PersonPronoun (IOP)Meaning
A míMeto me
A tiTeto you (informal)
A él / ella / ustedLeto him / her / you (formal)
A nosotros/asNosto us
A ellos / ellas / ustedesLesto them / you all

Rule 1: Singular subject → gusta

Instruction: If what you like is one thing (singular noun or one activity), use gusta.

Me gusta el café.
Te gusta la música.
Le gusta el libro.
Nos gusta la clase.

Rule 2: Plural subject → gustan

Instruction: If what you like is more than one thing (plural noun), use gustan. The verb agrees with the subject.

Me gustan los libros.
Te gustan las flores.
Les gustan los deportes.

Rule 3: Activities (verbs) → always gusta

Instruction: When you talk about an activity (infinitive: nadar, leer, bailar…), always use singular gusta, even with two verbs.

Me gusta viajar.
Me gusta escribir y leer.
¿Te gusta bailar?

Clarifiers: Who likes it?

Instruction: Le can mean "to him", "to her" or "to you (formal)". We often add a clarifier (a él, a ella, a usted) at the start.

ClarifierMeaning
A élto him
A ellato her
A ustedto you (formal)
A ellos / A ellasto them
A mi mamá le gusta el café. (To my mom, coffee is pleasing.)

Negative: No + pronoun

Instruction: To say "I don't like", put No before the pronoun. Same structure: No me gusta / No me gustan.

No me gusta el frío.
No nos gustan los exámenes.
¿No te gusta la música?

Summary: Me gusta

Quick reference: Pronoun (IOP) + gusta/gustan + subject. Singular or verb → gusta. Plural → gustan.
