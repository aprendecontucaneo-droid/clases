Common Gringo Mistakes

English speakers often make these errors when learning Spanish verbs.
Learn to avoid these common pitfalls and speak like a native!

Mistake #1

No "Do / Does"

Why it matters:

Spanish doesn't use auxiliary verbs like "do/does" for questions. The verb conjugation alone shows the question.

WRONG:

CORRECT:

¬øEstudias?

Mistake #2

Wrong Negation

Why it matters:

In Spanish, simply put "NO" before the verb. Don't use "don't" or "doesn't" - they don't exist!

WRONG:

I don't eat meat.

CORRECT:

Yo no como carne.

Mistake #3

Using Unnecessary Subjects

Why it matters:

Spanish verb endings tell us who's doing the action. You usually don't need to say "yo", "t√∫", "nosotros", etc.

SOUNDS UNNATURAL:

BETTER (Natural):

(Nosotros) Comemos pizza.

The ending "-emos" already tells us it's "we"!

Pro Tip for English Speakers:

Think of Spanish verbs as self-contained units that already include all the information. You don't need extra words like "do", "does", or "don't" - the verb ending does all the work! This makes Spanish simpler and more direct than English.
